# 1. Executive Summary

- **The Hook:**Stop losing money on disputes. Show your clients exactly what was done — automatically.

FieldOps AI is a workforce operations platform built for construction, electrical grid, and field service companies. It replaces fragmented tools — WhatsApp, Excel, paper logs, and basic time trackers — with one system built around a single powerful output: a complete, verified job timeline that proves the work.

Unlike generic SaaS tools, FieldOps AI is purpose-built for the realities of field teams: GPS-verified clock-ins, photo documentation with tamper-evident metadata, job-based time tracking, and automated reporting — all in a mobile-first interface workers can operate with gloves on.

## The Core Differentiator

- **One Thing:**The job timeline that proves the work. Every clock-in, photo, task completion, and supervisor note flows into a single chronological record — automatically generated, client-ready, and legally defensible. This is not a feature. It is the product.

Everything else — workforce management, operations, and AI intelligence — exists to make that timeline richer and more actionable. This focus is what separates FieldOps AI from both generic time trackers (no proof layer) and enterprise platforms like Procore (too complex, weeks to implement, not mobile-first).

## Strategic Origin

FieldOps AI begins as a real deployment for a single field operations company. This means the first version is built against live requirements, tested on real crews, and validated with actual payroll and client reporting workflows. That pilot becomes the foundation of a horizontal SaaS product — giving B&Br Technology a competitive moat competitors can't replicate: proven, production-grade software with a reference customer.

# 6. Core Features — MVP Scope

## 6.1 User & Role Management

- Multi-company architecture — single platform, isolated company data

- Role hierarchy: Admin → Supervisor → Worker, with per-role permissions

- Invite-based onboarding with deep link setup (workers need < 2 minutes to activate)

- Supervisor-scoped visibility (only sees assigned jobs and workers)

## 6.2 Time Tracking System

- One-tap clock in/out from mobile app

- GPS coordinate captured and stored at every clock event

- Geofencing: clock-in only permitted within configurable radius of job site

- Break start/end tracking with optional break type classification

- Automated overtime detection with supervisor alert

- Timesheet export (PDF and Excel) per worker, per job, per date range

## 6.3 Job & Project Management

Structure: Company → Projects → Jobs → Tasks

- Project: top-level grouping (e.g., City Grid Expansion Phase 2)

- Job: discrete unit of work with location, assigned crew, and schedule

- Task: individual checklist item within a job

- Worker assignment at job or task level

- Job status: Draft → Active → In Progress → Review → Completed

- Supervisor notes and client-visible updates

## 6.4 Photo Proof System — Stamped Evidence

Every photo is a self-contained legal artifact. A client receiving the PDF sees the proof burned directly onto the image itself — visible stamp, not hidden metadata.

- Photos captured in-app only — no gallery uploads ever. In-app capture is enforced at OS permission level.

- Burned-in proof stamp on every photo: company logo (top-left), worker full name and role, GPS coordinates, date and exact time, job reference code, and site name — applied server-side so the stamp cannot be removed without destroying the image

- The stamped image is the single source of truth: what appears on the dashboard thumbnail, in the project photo feed, in the PDF report, and in client-facing exports is always the same stamped image

- Before / After mode: two stamped photos paired side-by-side in reports for comparison documentation

- Required photo checkpoints per task type — worker cannot mark a task complete without the checkpoint photo

- Cryptographic hash logged at upload — any external modification of the image invalidates the hash, detectable on audit

- In-app photo gallery per job: supervisor sees every photo from every worker chronologically, filterable by worker, date range, and task type

- **Design Principle:**The stamp burned onto the image is what makes it usable in client disputes. A screenshot of the stamped photo carries the same visual proof as the original export. This is the feature that wins the sale.

- **Positioning:**Market as 'GPS-verified and metadata-secured with visual proof stamps.' Never claim 'tamper-proof' — accurate framing is legally defensible and still compelling to buyers.

## 6.5 Project Photo Feed

Every job has a shared, real-time photo feed — a chronological stream of all stamped photos from all crew members on that job. This is not a chat. It is a shared evidence wall visible to the entire project team in real time.

- All crew members and the supervisor on a job see every submitted photo in a live feed

- Each feed entry shows: stamped thumbnail, worker name, time submitted, and task tag

- Supervisor can comment on a photo, flag it for follow-up, or confirm it as a checkpoint

- Feed is submission-only for workers — they submit, supervisors annotate

- Offline-synced: photos queued locally appear in the feed automatically when connectivity returns

- The feed becomes the visual backbone of the final job completion report

## 6.6 OT Verification System

Overtime disputes are one of the most costly recurring problems in field operations. OT Verification turns overtime from a trust problem into a proof problem — and solves it with a dedicated photo-stamped approval workflow.

- When a worker reaches their shift time threshold, the app surfaces an OT prompt: 'You are entering overtime. Submit an OT verification photo to continue working.'

- Worker takes an in-app photo at the moment of overtime — stamped with exact time, GPS coordinates, job reference, and their name

- Supervisor receives an immediate push notification with the stamped photo, current job status, total hours to date, and Approve / Deny buttons

- Supervisor sees real visual proof before approving — not just a time entry

- Approval or denial is logged as an immutable OTApprovalEvent with the supervisor's name, decision, timestamp, and reason (optional)

- The OT photo appears in the job timeline, the project photo feed, and the payroll CSV export

- Unapproved OT is flagged red in the timesheet and the weekly admin report

- **Feature Value:**OT Verification is a named, marketable feature — not a sub-bullet of time tracking. It is the answer to every field company owner who has ever paid overtime they couldn't verify was real.

## 6.7 Smart Timeline

Every job generates a chronological event log automatically. This is the core audit object of the platform. All events are immutable — nothing is ever deleted or overwritten.

- Events: ClockEvent, PhotoEvent, OTApprovalEvent, TaskEvent, NoteEvent, CorrectionEvent, AlertEvent

- Correction model: supervisors never edit original events. To fix an error, they append a CorrectionEvent with the corrected value, their name, timestamp, and reason. The original event is preserved. The audit trail is never broken.

- Immutable event log becomes client proof document, payroll audit trail, dispute evidence, and Phase 3 AI training data

- Timeline view: mobile shows each worker's own history; web dashboard shows full job timeline with all crew events interleaved

## 6.8 Reporting Engine

- Auto-generated daily report per job — zero manual input required

- Job completion report: full stamped photo gallery, crew hours with OT breakdown, timeline of all events, task checklist status

- PDF export: stamped photos visible in the report itself — client receives undeniable visual proof with every page

- CSV/Excel export designed explicitly for bookkeeper use: worker name, date, regular hours, OT hours, OT approved by, job code — one row per worker per day. Bookkeeper can use it immediately without translation.

- Supervisor annotates and approves before client delivery

- Email delivery with company-branded template

## 6.9 Manager Dashboard

- Live map as the primary view — GPS pins for all clocked-in workers and job site markers. This is Phase 1, not Phase 2.

- Active jobs list: progress percentage, last photo submitted time, crew count, and alert flags

- OT approval queue: pending OT requests surface as priority alerts with one-tap review

- Hours tracker: total hours by worker, by job, by date range — with OT highlighted

- Alert feed: geofence violations, missed photo checkpoints, unapproved overtime, late arrivals

## 6.10 Mobile Worker App

Designed for the worst-case device: old Android on a construction site with poor signal. Minimum supported spec must be locked before build: Android 8.0+, iOS 13+, 2GB RAM, 1GB free storage. The 30-second rule applies to every daily worker action.

- Home screen above the fold: clock status (IN / OUT), current job name, today's task count, and pending OT status

- One-tap clock in/out — GPS captured in background, confirmation in under 2 seconds

- Camera flow: open camera → capture → submit → back to home in 3 taps, 10 seconds

- Project photo feed: worker sees all team photos on their job in real time

- OT prompt: automatic surface at shift threshold — worker cannot miss it

- Permanent tracking indicator on every screen: 'GPS active — clocked in' or 'Not tracking — clocked out.' Workers always know when they are being tracked.

- Work history: personal record of all jobs, hours logged, photos submitted

- **Critical:**Multilingual worker app is a Phase 2 requirement, not a Phase 4 nice-to-have. For West Africa, Middle East, and Southeast Asia deployments, the worker app must be in the worker's own language. French, Arabic, and Thai translations are low-effort given the app's simplicity — but adoption drops significantly when a worker navigates in a language they don't read fluently.

## 6.11 Foreman App — A Separate Experience

The foreman running a 12-person crew on site has a fundamentally different daily workflow from both the individual worker and the office admin. Competitive analysis of Trayd and field operator feedback confirms this deserves its own screen design — not just a filtered version of the supervisor dashboard. This is a Phase 2 addition.

- Home view: crew attendance at a glance — who is clocked in, who is late, who has not arrived

- One-tap flag: foreman marks any worker event, photo, or task for admin review without leaving the current screen

- Crew clock-in override: foreman can clock in a worker who forgot — their name is attached as the approver, creating an immutable audit entry

- Daily shift log entry: structured text record of the shift — headcount, tasks completed, issues encountered, materials used, message to the PM

- OT approval from the foreman home screen — one-tap Approve/Deny with photo visible

- Simplified job status update: one-tap status change (In Progress → Under Review → Complete) with optional note

## 6.12 Daily Shift Report

Distinct from the job completion PDF and the event timeline, the Daily Shift Report is the foreman's structured professional log — the document a PM uses to track the day, accounting uses to verify labour, and clients sometimes request. This is a named standalone feature, not an export option. Trayd's product analysis confirms this is the most referenced daily document across field operations workflows.

- Auto-populated from the day's events: headcount, hours logged, photos submitted, tasks completed, incidents flagged

- Foreman adds a structured summary: what was accomplished today, what is blocked, what is next

- Distributed automatically at end of day: supervisor receives push notification, admin receives email

- Archives as a standalone document per job per day — searchable and downloadable independently of the full job PDF

- Becomes the structured data input for AI-generated summaries in Phase 3

## 6.13 Offline Mode

- Full clock in/out and photo capture without any internet connection

- Offline photo cap: 50 photos per device per sync cycle to prevent storage overflow on low-end devices

- Queue-based sync: events upload in strict chronological order on reconnect — no duplicates, no conflicts

- Conflict model: append-only events mean sync conflicts are architecturally impossible — every event gets a server-assigned timestamp on upload

- Sync status always visible: 'All synced', 'Uploading 3 photos...', or 'Offline — 5 events queued'

## 7.10 Certified Payroll Reporting (US Public Works)

Any US contractor working on a government-funded project — public schools, infrastructure, municipal buildings — is legally required to submit certified payroll reports weekly. Without this feature, FieldOps AI cannot be used on the most lucrative jobs in the US market. This is a Phase 4 US market unlock, not optional.

- Automated certified payroll reports generated directly from job-level time and wage records

- Worker classifications, prevailing wage rates, fringe benefits, and hours by trade pulled from the ClockEvent task_classification field

- Direct submission integrations for major compliance platforms: Elation Systems, LCPtracker, eComply, and state-level systems

- Complete audit trail: every submission stored alongside the underlying payroll and workforce records for auditor access

- **Why Phase 1 Schema Matters:**This feature is only possible because task_classification was added to ClockEvent in Phase 1. ## 7.11 Union Management & Prevailing Wage

- Union job title management: workers hold multiple classifications (e.g. Journeyman Electrician, Foreman, Helper) — active classification selectable per shift

- Shift premiums: automatic rate modifiers applied for hazard pay, night differentials, holiday rates, and multi-state work

- Task code surcharges: specific tasks trigger rate changes automatically — no manual payroll adjustments

- Prevailing wage rate tables: configurable by project, jurisdiction, and trade

## 8.2 Data Architecture

Everything in FieldOps AI is stored as an event. This is the foundational design principle.

- ClockEvent: worker_id, job_id, timestamp, gps_lat, gps_lng, device_id, type (IN/OUT/BREAK_START/BREAK_END), task_classification (nullable)

- PhotoEvent: worker_id, job_id, task_id, timestamp, gps_lat, gps_lng, image_url, hash, watermark_url

- TaskEvent: task_id, worker_id, timestamp, status, notes

- NoteEvent: author_id, job_id, timestamp, content, visibility (internal/client)

- AlertEvent: type, triggered_by, job_id, worker_id, timestamp, resolved_at

- ShiftReportEvent: foreman_id, job_id, date, headcount, summary, blocked_items, next_steps

- OTApprovalEvent: worker_id, job_id, approver_id, decision, photo_url, timestamp, reason

- **Critical Schema Note:**task_classification is a nullable field on ClockEvent added in Phase 1 and used in Phase 4. It captures the task`code`or trade classification a worker was performing during a clock period — required for union prevailing wage calculations, certified payroll reporting, and US public works compliance. Adding it now costs nothing. Retrofitting it into thousands of existing events later is expensive and error-prone. This is a schema decision, not a feature decision.

This event-sourced approach enables: timeline generation, audit trails, AI processing, real-time dashboards, and retrospective analytics — all from the same data layer.

## 8.3 Multi-Tenancy Design

- Each company is a tenant with isolated data via Supabase Row Level Security (RLS)

- Supervisors see only their assigned jobs and workers

- Workers see only their own timeline and assignments

- Admins have full company-scoped access; no cross-company visibility

- White-label clients get a branded subdomain with their own admin access

# 9. UI/UX Design Direction

## 12.3 Before You Write Code — Operational Prerequisites

- **Non-Negotiable:**These five items must be completed before the first sprint begins. Skipping any one of them will cause a preventable failure during the pilot.

- Signed pilot agreement: scope, timeline, Phase 1 success metric (80-90% of crew clock in and submit at least one photo per shift for 30 straight days without babysitting), and what the pilot company provides if successful — case study, logo use, reference calls, and 3 warm introductions

- 'We may change or remove features during the pilot' clause in the agreement — this prevents scope politics and lets you iterate

- Support runbook: who answers when clock-in fails at 7:10am. Define the top 10 failure modes and the response for each: no GPS signal, no data connection, wrong job selected, app crash, forgot password, OT prompt confusion, sync errors, photo upload failure, geofence false positive, device storage full. Build a WhatsApp group: you + supervisor + one admin contact.

- Device minimum spec locked: Android 8.0+, iOS 13+, 2GB RAM, 1GB free storage, rear camera functional, location permissions grantable. Test on the oldest device your pilot crew actually uses — not your development phone.

- Founder metrics instrumented from Day 1: events per day, DAU/WAU, median time-to-clock-in, photos per worker per shift, OT verification completion rate, sync failure rate. If you don't measure adoption from Day 1, you fly blind into every decision.

## 12.4 Tech Debt Guardrails

- Row-level security in Supabase in place before any multi-tenant data is stored — no exceptions

- Photo storage via signed, expiring URLs only — never expose raw storage paths

- GPS validation server-side — client GPS coordinates are advisory, server validates against job site radius

- Offline queue designed before Phase 1 ships — retrofitting the sync model is expensive and error-prone

- Photo stamp applied server-side — never client-side. Client-side stamp can be bypassed; server-side stamp cannot.

- UI strings externalised in Flutter from Day 1 using the intl package — never hardcode text in widgets. This is a 2-hour setup that unlocks multilingual support in Phase 2 without any code refactoring.

- task_classification field added to ClockEvent schema in Phase 1 as nullable — used in Phase 4 for certified payroll and union wage calculations. Missing this field means Phase 4 certified payroll cannot be built without a data migration.

- AI cost budget per company tier: Starter plan gets a hard cap of 20 AI report generations per month. Pro gets 100. Business gets unlimited. Without this, one heavy user on the $79 plan can cost more than their subscription in API calls.

# 13. Risk Analysis & Mitigation

- **Design Law:**Rule: If a worker cannot complete their daily clock-in and photo submission in under 30 seconds, the product fails — regardless of how good everything else is. Adoption is the #1 risk. Not technology. Not competitors.

| - **Risk** | - **Level** | - **Mitigation** |
| - **Field workers resist adopting the app (#1 Risk)** | - **CRITICAL** | 30-second rule governs every worker-facing design decision. On-site onboarding session with full crew before pilot goes live. Support runbook ready for top 10 failure modes at 7am on Day 1. Worker-facing privacy indicator ('GPS only when clocked in') must be prominent and honest. |
| Pilot company changes requirements mid-build | - **High** | Signed SOW with explicit 'features may change during pilot' clause. All change requests`go`to a backlog. Scope is locked for Phase 1. |
| Offline sync corrupts or loses events | - **High** | Append-only event model — events are never overwritten. CorrectionEvents are the only way to amend records. Offline cap of 50 photos prevents device storage overflow on low-end devices. |
| SMB owners hear 'proof' as 'legally bulletproof' | - **High** | Explicit 'evidence, not guarantee' stance in app UI, ToS, and marketing copy. Product is described as 'GPS-verified and metadata-secured visual evidence.' Include a one-page policy template companies can share with their workers and clients. |
| Old Android devices break GPS or camera permissions | - **High** | Minimum device spec locked before build. Test on the oldest device in the pilot crew's hands, not the dev phone. Graceful degradation: if GPS is unavailable, app prompts worker to enable it before clock-in is allowed. |
| AI cost exceeds revenue on Starter tier | - **Medium** | Hard per-tier AI call caps: Starter 20 reports/mo, Pro 100/mo, Business unlimited. Enforce at the API gateway. Heavy users are a conversion opportunity — prompt upgrade rather than silently eating cost. |

# 14. Security & Compliance

## 14.1 Data Security

- All data in transit encrypted via HTTPS/TLS 1.3

- All data at rest encrypted (Supabase default AES-256)

- Photos stored in private buckets — served via signed, expiring URLs only

- Row-level security in PostgreSQL prevents cross-company data access at the database layer

- JWT tokens expire and rotate; refresh token rotation enabled

## 14.2 Privacy

- Workers see only their own data; no lateral visibility to other worker timelines

- Supervisors see only workers and jobs within their company

- GPS tracking only active during clock-in state — not continuous background tracking

- Privacy policy must explicitly define when location is captured and stored

- GDPR-compatible: right to export and right to delete (soft delete with 90-day purge)

## 14.3 Compliance Design

- OSHA-compatible incident log export in Phase 3+

- Audit log for all admin actions (role changes, data exports, job deletions)

- Data retention policies configurable per company (default 3 years)

- E-signature support for job completion sign-off (Phase 3)
